3-Paragraph Movie Rants

Inception: Dream or Nightmare?

Christopher Nolan’s *Inception* would be a fantastic premise for a video game--perhaps an MMORPG for all I know about that industry. I mean, I might even play that game. However, as a movie, it's pretentious, convoluted, and completely lacking in emotional involvement. I find it frustrating to see actresses as gifted as Marion Cotillard be caught up in the hollow spectacle of endless machine gun fire and zero-gravity fist-fights.

Perhaps the reason the characters repeatedly say "We have to go deeper" is Nolan's subconscious awareness that his characters, storyline, and even concept lacks depth. Remember that last dream you had? Was it straight out of Mission Impossible, or was it a strange blend of people you know, emotions you feel, and a bunch of odd, arbitrary details? As Freud postulated, people often dream about repressed, intense needs like sex, trauma, emotional yearning, or practically any other psychological unrest. A train going down a street? Maybe, but that's like a 2 out of 10 on the dream weirdness scale. *Inception* needs more *Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind* and *Everything Everywhere All At Once* and less *John Wick* and *The Town*

By the time the top is spinning at the end, I found myself still respecting Nolan's ambition, even though he fails to make *Inception* resonate any further than a shallow, popcorn-munching action flick can resonate. In 2023,*Oppenheimer* benefited from a heightening of emotional stakes and a lack of rotating hallways.

The Dark Knight: Cinema's Apex

*The Dark Knight* isn’t just a superhero movie—it’s a full-fledged crime epic. Heath Ledger’s Joker is chaotic perfection, commanding every scene he’s in.

The pacing, the intensity, the action—it all builds into one of the greatest movies ever made. Even Christian Bale’s sometimes silly Batman voice can’t diminish the impact.

It’s a movie that’s endlessly rewatchable. The cinematography, the dialogue, the performances—it all comes together in an almost flawless way.
